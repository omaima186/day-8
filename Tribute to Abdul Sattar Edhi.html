Tribute to Abdul Sattar Edhi

Why Abdul Sattar Edhi is Admired

Abdul Sattar Edhi was a renowned Pakistani philanthropist and humanitarian. His dedication to helping the needy and marginalized communities in Pakistan has made him a symbol of selflessness and compassion.

Born in 1928, Edhi started his journey by establishing the Edhi Foundation, which provided a wide range of social services, including healthcare, emergency relief, orphanages, and shelters for the homeless. He personally embraced a simple lifestyle, wearing modest clothes and living in a small apartment adjacent to his foundation's office.

Throughout his life, Edhi received numerous international awards for his humanitarian work, including the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Public Service and the Nishan-e-Imtiaz, Pakistan's highest civilian honor. His commitment to humanity transcended borders, as he extended his support to victims of natural disasters and refugees beyond Pakistan's borders.

Abdul Sattar Edhi's legacy continues to inspire millions around the world. His motto, "Live and help live," reflects his belief in the power of compassion and the importance of serving humanity without discrimination.
